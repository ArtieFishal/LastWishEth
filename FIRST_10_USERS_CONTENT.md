# LastWish First 10 Users Content Pack

These are meant to get feedback, trust, and first users, not empty impressions.

## Post 1, the core problem
If something happened to you tomorrow, would your family know:
- which wallets you used
- what assets you held
- how to access them
- what you wanted done

Most crypto holders have no real inheritance plan.

That is the problem LastWish is built to solve.

## Post 2, trust angle
LastWish is built around one rule:
we do **not** store seed phrases or private keys.

It is not a custodian.
It does not hold funds.
It helps you create clear inheritance instructions your family or executor can actually follow.

## Post 3, emotional realism
A lot of people say they are “early” in crypto.
Very few are prepared for what happens if they disappear.

If your system only works while you are alive and available, it is not really a system.

## Post 4, practical framing
Traditional estate planning usually does not capture the crypto-specific details that matter:
- wallet addresses
- chain context
- NFT holdings
- how your executor should think about access

That gap is where families get stuck.

## Post 5, ask for feedback
I’m looking for a few crypto holders to give brutally honest feedback on LastWish.

It is a tool for creating crypto inheritance instructions without storing keys.

If you have wallets across chains and want to test the flow, I would love sharp feedback.

## Post 6, spouse/executor angle
The real user is not just the crypto holder.
It is also the spouse, child, sibling, or executor who would be left trying to figure out a maze of wallets and assets.

Crypto inheritance is a family problem, not just a wallet problem.

## Post 7, professional angle
I think crypto inheritance is a real opening for attorneys and advisors.

Not because they need to become on-chain experts overnight, but because clients need a clearer bridge between estate planning and wallet reality.

That is a gap worth fixing.

## Post 8, anti-spreadsheet angle
A spreadsheet is better than nothing.
But “better than nothing” is a low bar for a high-stakes situation.

This category needs:
- clarity
- structure
- safer habits
- documents people can actually follow

## Post 9, simple CTA
If you hold meaningful crypto and your family would be confused without you, try LastWish and tell me where the trust breaks.

That feedback is more valuable than compliments.

## Post 10, founder-style honesty
I’m not trying to build a hype product here.
I’m trying to solve a painfully real crypto problem:
people leave behind wallets, assets, and confusion.

If LastWish is going to matter, it has to earn trust first.
