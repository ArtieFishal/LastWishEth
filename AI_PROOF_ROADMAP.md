# LastWish AI-Proof Roadmap

## Core truth

If LastWish stays as only a page that says "AI can help you make a document," it can be replaced.

If LastWish becomes the most trusted, most practical, most family-usable **crypto legacy workflow**, it gets harder to replace.

The moat is not "having AI."
The moat is:
- trust
- workflow depth
- family usability
- professional adoption
- content authority
- recurring update behavior
- brand in a scary category where people want a real guide

## The right AI-proofing strategy

Use AI to build faster in 5 directions:

1. **Better product guidance**
2. **Better conversion and education**
3. **Better recurring user value**
4. **Better professional workflows**
5. **Better distribution and authority**

## What makes LastWish hard to replace

### 1. Trust moat
Generic AI can generate words.
It cannot easily become the trusted name people think of for crypto inheritance.

Build trust through:
- clear security stance
- family-first explanations
- sample documents
- better FAQs
- professional positioning
- no-hype brand voice
- repeatable yearly update process

### 2. Workflow moat
The product should solve the full messy reality, not just output text.

That means helping with:
- wallet discovery
- asset organization
- beneficiary clarity
- executor instructions
- family handoff logic
- printable and lawyer-friendly output
- annual updates

### 3. Relationship moat
The long-term winners will have relationships with:
- estate attorneys
- tax/accounting professionals
- crypto advisors
- family offices
- influencers trusted by self-custody users

### 4. Content moat
Own the niche by becoming the clearest teacher in crypto legacy planning.

### 5. Recurring behavior moat
If LastWish becomes something users revisit every 6 to 12 months, it turns from a one-time tool into a living category leader.

## Product features to add

## A. Conversational Legacy Interview
An AI-guided intake that helps the user think through:
- what wallets exist
- what assets matter
- who needs to know what
- where secrets should and should not be stored
- what their executor would actually need

This should feel like a calm interview, not a chatbot gimmick.

## B. Family Readiness Score
A simple score like:
- undocumented
- partially prepared
- executor-ready

This is good for:
- conversion
- lead magnets
- annual updates
- shareable education

## C. Executor Packet Generator
Generate a simpler packet specifically for the person who may need to act.

Possible outputs:
- what exists
- where to start
- what not to do
- who to contact
- which documents to find

## D. Lawyer Handoff Packet
A cleaner professional-facing version for attorneys or advisors.

## E. Annual Update Agent
A yearly or quarterly reminder flow:
- did wallets change?
- new chains?
- new assets?
- new beneficiaries?
- new executor?

This can become a subscription or premium retention layer.

## F. Sample Document Preview
This is a major trust feature.
People need to see what "good" looks like before they pay.

## G. Concierge Review Layer
Offer human-plus-AI review for premium users.
This is one of the strongest monetization and moat moves.

## H. Professional Mode
A dedicated workflow for attorneys, advisors, and family offices.
